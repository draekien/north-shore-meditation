# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three audiences share the site, in roughly equal weight:

- **Curious newcomers** — first-time visitors with little or no prior exposure to Transcendental Meditation. They are exploring what TM is, who Grace is, and whether an intro talk is worth their time. Often arriving via search, a friend's recommendation, or local word-of-mouth on Sydney's North Shore.
- **Considered buyers** — already TM-aware, evaluating Grace specifically against other certified teachers. They need credibility signals (lineage, qualifications, longevity), a clear sense of how Grace teaches, schedule visibility, and pricing transparency.
- **Existing students and community** — returning practitioners who use the site as an ongoing companion: reading the journal, booking refreshers and group meditations, following events, sharing pages with friends and family.

Visitors arrive on phones and laptops in equal measure, often outside any "work" context — at home, in the evening, between commitments. The site is read in moments of consideration, not transaction.

## Product Purpose

Make Grace the recognisable, trusted TM teacher on Sydney's North Shore — and make the site a living, deepening body of work that returning students rely on.

Bookings are not the headline metric. Trust and journal depth are. The site succeeds when a visitor leaves understanding who Grace is, why TM is taught the way it is, and feels invited rather than sold to. Conversions follow from that, not from CTAs.

The site is bilingual (English and 简体中文) and treats both locales as first-class.

## Brand Personality

**Warm. Encouraging. Gentle.**

Voice is that of a generous teacher speaking to an adult: plain, considered, unhurried. Never preachy, never reverent, never urgent. The site should feel like the practice it teaches — slow enough to breathe in, generous enough to come back to.

Emotionally the site evokes two things at once: **calm and spacious** (room to think, ample air, soft pacing) and **warm and human** (Grace's presence, photographs of real people, the texture of a real teaching practice on a real street).

Copy is sparing. One clear sentence beats three eloquent ones.

## Anti-references

Five things this site should explicitly not look or sound like:

- **Generic wellness-app SaaS.** No Calm/Headspace marketing tropes — no pastel gradient blobs, no friendly rounded geometry trying to feel like an app, no consumer-app sheen.
- **New-age / mystical cliché.** No mandalas, lotus icons, chakra colour wheels, purple-cosmos imagery, or AI-generated "serene" stock photography. No Sanskrit set in decorative display fonts as ornament.
- **Corporate-startup template.** No hero-metric grid, no identical feature card rows, no navy-and-blue tech palette, no "trusted by" logo strip styling, no SaaS landing-page reflexes.
- **Heavy spiritual / religious framing.** TM is taught here as a secular technique. Copy and imagery must not read as devotional, ashram-marketing, or culty. Lineage is honoured as history, not invoked as authority.
- **Generic meditation website.** Soft-focus stock of someone meditating on a beach at sunset, "find your inner peace" headlines, gradient lotus logos. If a visitor could swap the logo for any other meditation studio, we have failed.

## Design Principles

Five strategic principles guide every design decision on the site:

1. **Teacher first, technique second.** Grace's voice and presence ground every page. TM's legitimacy comes through her — through how she explains it, the people she has taught, the lineage she comes from — not through abstract claims about benefits.
2. **Stillness is content.** Pace, whitespace, and unhurried rhythm are part of the message. The site should feel like the practice it teaches. Density and busy-ness are not neutral defaults; they actively contradict the brand.
3. **Plain language over reverence.** Explain TM the way you would to an intelligent friend over coffee. No mysticism, no jargon, no honorifics in body copy. Demystify without flattening.
4. **Bilingual parity.** English and 简体中文 surfaces share the same craft, the same pacing, the same considered typography. Chinese is never an afterthought translation pasted into an English-shaped layout.
5. **Earn trust before asking.** Depth — the journal, Grace's story, the lineage, the qualifications — comes before any booking ask. The CTA is what a visitor reaches for once persuaded, not what we push at them.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Commit to **WCAG 2.2 AA** across the site:

- Colour contrast meets AA for all text and meaningful UI.
- Full keyboard navigation with visible, considered focus states (focus is part of the design, not an afterthought).
- All imagery has alt text; decorative imagery is marked as such.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` is respected globally; existing animated background gradients and blobs must downgrade gracefully to a static state.
- Bilingual support (en / zh-Hans) is held to the same craft standard in both locales — typography, line length, and reading rhythm are tuned for each.
